Synthesizer

A digital synthesizer built in C# that reads sheet music files and plays them back using additive synthesis — combining sine and square waves to create complex tones.

Waveform visualization showing additive synthesis in action.

What it is

A synthesizer that takes MusicXML files (a standard format for sheet music) and plays them back by generating audio from scratch. No samples, no MIDI — it builds every sound from raw waveforms using additive synthesis.

You feed it a MusicXML file, it parses the musical score into notes with timing, and then generates audio by combining sine and square waves at the right frequencies and durations.

How it works

  • MusicXML Parser — Custom parser reads the XML-based sheet music format, extracting notes, durations, dynamics, and timing.
  • Score → Notes — Converts the parsed musical score into a sequence of playable notes with precise timing.
  • Additive Synthesis — Each note is built by combining multiple waveforms (sine, square) at harmonically related frequencies to create the desired timbre.
  • WAV Output — The mathematical wave representations get sampled and written to WAV format for playback.
